Motivational Speech Synthesis

Text-to-motivational-speech with adjustable motivational factor to control motivational prosody

Motivational speech has emerged as a popular audiovisual phenomenon within Western subcultures, conveying optimal strategies and principles for success through expressive, high-energy delivery. The present paper artistically explores methods for synthesizing the distinctive prosodic patterns inherent to motivational speech, while critically examining its sociocultural foundations. Drawing on recent advances in emotion-controllable text-to-speech (TTS) systems and speech emotion recognition (SER), we employ deep learning models and frameworks to replicate and analyze this genre of speech. Within our proposed architecture, we introduce a one-dimensional motivational factor derived from high-dimensional emotional speech representations, enabling the control of motivational prosody according to intensity. Situated within broader discourses on self-optimization and meritocracy, Motivational Speech Synthesis contributes to the field of emotional speech synthesis, while also prompting reflection on the societal values embedded in such mediated narratives.

Proposed architecture

The following diagram illustrates the architecture developed for synthesizing motivational speech building ontop the EmoKnob model. Motivational intensity is controlled via averaged speaker embeddings, derived by selecting and averaging speech samples corresponding to different levels of motivational intensity from our dataset. These embeddings are generated in discrete increments along our one-dimensional motivational factor, ranging from 0 (low intensity) to 1 (high intensity). During inference, the closest embedding is selected according to the desired motivational factor, enabling fine-grained emotional control of synthesized speech.

Motivational Factor

The visualization below shows a 3-dimensional representation of emotional speech samples extracted from motivational speeches, projected into a Valence-Arousal-Dominance (VAD) space. Each point represents a motivational speech segment collected from YouTube, embedded using a deep-learning-based emotion recognition model (Wav2Vec). Using the UMAP dimensionality reduction algorithm, these complex emotional patterns are compressed into a single value, called the motivational factor, which ranges from 0 (low motivational intensity) to 1 (high motivational intensity). Explore the interactive plot to see how emotional dimensions correspond to the motivational intensity scale.

Even though Motivational Speech Synthesis strives for artistic reflection, we want to emphasize, that this project does not aim to judge any person actually benefiting from motivational speech or similar phenomena. We don't expose or look at people consuming motivational speech, but rather focus on deconstructing underlying circumstances and attitudes of those narratives, which arrive as symptoms of a society driven by growth and success.
